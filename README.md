## 🌐 The HJS Technical Implementation Framework

### *Objective Evidence Chain for Global AI Intelligence*

HJS (Human Judgment System) provides a standardized **implementation method** to anchor AI outputs into verifiable evidence. We recognize the intelligence captured by mainstream protocols (OpenAI, Anthropic, ISO) and provide the technical layer to "solidify" these digital actions into a compliance chain that meets the objective requirements of the EU AI Act.

### 1. Mathematical Implementation: Data Integrity

* **Mechanism**: **Ed25519 (RFC 8032)** Edwards-curve Digital Signature Algorithm.
* **Function**: This method provides a mathematical seal for AI intelligence. By implementing asynchronous signatures, we ensure that the operational results of any AI system remain immutable. This moves the audit process from "subjective trust" to "mathematical verification."

### 2. Physical Implementation: Temporal Traceability

* **Mechanism**: **RFC 9562 (UUIDv7)** Millisecond-Precision Sequencing.
* **Function**: This method anchors the fluid nature of AI tool-calls into physical time. By hardcoding Unix-epoch timestamps into every judgment identifier, HJS provides a monotonic, irreversible sequence of events—providing the "Temporal Fact" required for **Art. 12** record-keeping.

### 3. Sovereign Implementation: Jurisdictional Compliance

* **Mechanism**: **Decentralized Sidecar Deployment**.
* **Function**: This method provides a technical way to respect digital borders. By allowing sovereign entities (such as EUSAiR) to host their own "Logic Gateways," the HJS protocol ensures that global AI intelligence operates under local legal logic without the need for data to leave the jurisdiction.
